About

I am a doctoral researcher at the Halle Institute for Economic Research (IWH) in Halle (Saale), Germany. I hold an M.Sc. in Economics from the London School of Economics and Political Science, and a B.A. in Economics and LL.B. in Law from Universidad de los Andes in Bogotá, Colombia.

My research focuses on labor markets, firm dynamics, industrial organization, and trade, with a particular interest in how policy and institutional factors shape market outcomes.

Research

Work in Progress

Plant Acquisitions and Labor Market Power: The Case of France's Loi Florange

France's Florange Law, enacted in 2014, requires companies employing at least 1,000 workers to actively seek potential buyers before proceeding with site closures. I exploit plausibly exogenous variation induced by this legislative size threshold to analyze the effect of plant acquisitions on downstream labor market outcomes.

Intellectual Property, Knowledge Diffusion and Business Dynamism

with Magnuson, Mertens, Miranda

Using scraped patent data and balance sheet data for French manufacturing firms, we show that reduced knowledge diffusion within industry, measured by lower rates of self-citations per patent, causes increased industry concentration and lower labor reallocation, firm entry and young-firm employment shares.

The Role of Data Investments in Firm Export Dynamics

with Magnuson

Using LinkedIn employee compensation and French administrative datasets, we show that data investments increase the likelihood of firms entering and staying in exporting, and they increase and stabilize export revenues. Simulations show that data productivity leads to faster foreign market entry and greater industry concentration.
